Erino was born in early Summer 2014 out of a roommate's antics.

One day, he began to append "erino" to everything he said. Sentences began to contain numerous unnecessary syllables. Quentin Tarantino became Quentinerino Tarantinoerino. Realizing the ridiculous nature of this language, I set out to write a simple translator.

The result is Translaterino - an attempt to bridge the gap between English and the whole brevity thing.

Erino is a simple language that appends to English words.

There are three pillars to the Erino language:

1. English words can be translated if they are at least four characters long.

2. Contractions, prepositions, and articles are not translated for brevity.

3. APPEND ERINO
